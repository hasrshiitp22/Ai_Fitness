Yoga Poses For Your Problem


~Sphinx Pose(Salamba Bhujangasana)

Strengthens spine, improves posture, relieves back pain, gentle therapeutic backbend.

How to Practice Salamba Bhujangasana:

1. Lie on your stomach, legs extended, tops of feet down.

2. Place elbows under shoulders, forearms parallel, palms flat.

3. Inhale, gently lift chest, keeping hips and pelvis grounded.

4. Engage core, relax shoulders away from ears.

5. Hold for 5-8 breaths, lengthening the spine.

6. Exhale, lower down slowly.

~Child’s Pose(Balasana)

Stretches spine, relieves back pain, reduces stress, improves flexibility, and calms mind.

How to Practice Balasana:

1. Kneel on the mat, big toes touching, knees hip-width apart.

2. Sit back onto heels, exhale as you fold forward.

3. Extend arms forward or rest them alongside your body.

4. Rest forehead on the mat, relax shoulders, and breathe deeply.

5. Hold for 30 seconds to 3 minutes.

~Legs-Up-the-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani)

Relieves spinal pressure, improves circulation, reduces swelling, and calms the mind.

How to Practice Paschimottanasana:

1. Sit sideways with hips close to a wall.

2. Swing legs up as you lie back, resting shoulders on the floor.

3. Adjust hips near the wall (or use a cushion for support).

4. Relax arms by your sides, palms up. Close your eyes.

5. Breathe deeply, hold 5–10 minutes.
