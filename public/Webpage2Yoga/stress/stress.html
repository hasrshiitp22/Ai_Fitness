Yoga Poses For Your Problem



~Corpse Pose(Savasana)

Savasana (Corpse Pose) is a powerful yoga practice to calm the nervous system and release stress.

How to Practice Savasana:

1. Lie down on your back, legs slightly apart, arms relaxed, palms up.

2. Close your eyes, soften your jaw, and release tension from forehead to toes.

3. Breathe naturally, letting each exhale melt away stress.

4. Stay still for 5–15 mins, focusing only on relaxation (no fidgeting!).

5. Exit gently—wiggle fingers/toes, stretch, and roll to your side before sitting up.

~Kapalbhati

Kapalbhati reduces stress by cleansing toxins, boosting oxygen, and calming the mind through rhythmic breathing.

How to Practice Kapalbhati:

1. Sit in Padmasana/Sukhasana with a straight spine.

2. Take a deep inhale, then forcefully exhale through the nose (belly pulls in).

3. Let inhalation happen passively (no effort).

4. Repeat quickly (60-100 pumps/min) for 1-3 rounds (30 sec to 1 min each).

5. Pause, breathe normally, observe calmness.

~Seated Forward Bend(Paschimottanasana)

Paschimottanasana reduces stress by calming the mind, stretching the spine, and activating relaxation through deep forward bending.

How to Practice Paschimottanasana:

1. Sit with legs extended straight, feet flexed.

2. Inhale, raise arms, lengthen spine.

3. Exhale, hinge from hips, fold forward.

4. Grasp feet/shins (keep knees slightly bent if needed).

5. Relax head/neck, hold for 30 sec–2 min with steady breathing.

6. Inhale to release slowly.
